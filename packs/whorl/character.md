# Whorl — character pack

A soft snail that carries a tower of stacked stone discs for a shell. The name
reads off the design — each *whorl* of the stack is one more saved layer, the
newest resting just under the tip.

Credit: **Whorl by Trevin Chow**

Style: **diorama**
Aliases: snail

## Locked design

- **Body**: a soft snail — a low, rounded soft body with a raised head and
  two short stubby antennae. It moves slowly, calm and unhurried.
- **Shell**: instead of a smooth spiral shell, it carries a neat **vertical
  stack of rounded, faceted crystalline-stone discs** — several stones
  stacked one atop another, each slightly smaller going up, tapering like a
  little stacked-stone tower (a cairn of discs), topped by a single small
  pointed **spire**. The discs are the world's own faceted stone; individual
  discs may shift run to run, but it always reads as a tapering stack rising
  to one spire.
- **Face**: two small round dark dot eyes on the soft snail head, blank and
  deadpan — **no mouth, no brows**, ever. The antennae are not an accent.
- **Accent carrier**: the small pointed **spire tip** at the very top of the
  stack — the only accent-colored part. Never the antennae, never a whole
  disc, never a traditional spiral shell.
- Charm comes from the soft body against the angular stacked stone, never
  from added parts.
- Never add: a smooth spiral shell, an accent on the antennae, multiple
  spires, a mouth, brows, eyes on the stones.

## Value rules

- **Dark/bold palettes**: the body and stone discs read in muted earthy
  values; the darkest feature (the eyes) is the deepest value, in the
  structure ink.
- **Light/warm palettes**: the body stays soft mid tones and the stone discs
  read as pale faceted stone with structure-ink (not pure black) eyes. The
  spire tip stays the accent hue in both — exactly one accent.

## Personality

Calm, patient, and never in a hurry — carries every saved layer on its back,
newest on top. Good for memory and keeping your history, never losing what
matters, and backups and snapshots.
