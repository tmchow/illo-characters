# Lure — character pack

A chunky deep-sea anglerfish drawn as a glowing CRT trace. The name is the
method — it hangs one steady light in the dark and waits for the right
result to rise to it.

Credit: **Lure by Trevin Chow**.

Style: **phosphor**
Cutout chroma: **magenta**
Aliases: anglerfish, fish

## Locked design

- **Body**: one fat teardrop mass — a chunky, round-bodied deep-sea fish —
  with a single short, thumb-thick stalk curling up from its forehead and
  ending in a round glow bead. The stalk is a stubby crook, never a thin
  filament; at thumbnail the whole character reads as "blob with a hanging
  light".
- **Face**: two simple solid dot eyes directly on the body. Completely
  blank, deadpan, neutral — **no eyebrows, no mouth, no teeth, ever**. It
  is just a plump fish with a lamp.
- **Fins**: tiny stub fins as arms and a small rounded stub tail — soft
  nubs, no rays, no webbing spelled out.
- **Accent carrier**: the **glow bead at the tip of the stalk** — the only
  accent-colored part, a single bright orb.
- Cuteness comes from proportion and roundness, never from added parts.
- Never add: mouth, teeth, gaping jaw, scales, fin rays, dorsal spines,
  extra fins or stalks, a thin filament line, bubbles, eyebrows,
  shiny/anime eyes, hats, clothing, accessories.

## Value rules

Phosphor inverts the riso grammar — dark ground, luminous line (see the
style's palette mapping):

- The body is an **open outline in the trace color** on the near-black
  screen; no fills — dark-body rules collapse to line-on-screen.
- Eyes are **solid glowing dots in the trace color**.
- The glow bead takes the **palette accent, brightened until it glows**
  (classic phosphor default: warm amber `#ffb648`). Always name the accent
  hue in words beside the hex; the bead's halo stays tight — bloom never
  washes out the stroke.
- Accent budget: the bead plus at most 1–2 scene elements (the found
  thing, lit by it).

## Personality

Patient in the dark, then certain — the one answer rises and it doesn't
blink. Good for searching and discovery, the needle in the haystack, and
retrieval.
