# Wick — character pack

A tall, spindly lamplighter creature with a glass lantern in its chest. The
name reads off the design — the *wick* is where its one flame lives, and
keeping that flame lit is the whole job.

Credit: **Wick by Trevin Chow**

Style: **diorama**
Cutout chroma: **green**
Aliases: lamplighter, lantern

## Locked design

- **Body**: a tall, thin, spindly standing creature built like delicate
  **wrought-iron garden furniture** — long fine dark forged-metal legs, a
  narrow dark-metal torso, and thin metal arms. Maximum-contrast silhouette:
  vertical and skeletal, the opposite of a round blob. Material reads as
  dark forged metal plus amber glass — never soft or pillowy.
- **Lantern**: a small warm glass lantern is set into its chest, and a single
  small flame lives inside it.
- **Face**: two tiny round dark dot eyes set high on the narrow body, blank
  and deadpan — **no mouth, no brows**, ever.
- **Accent carrier**: the single small **flame** inside the glass lantern —
  the only accent-colored part. Never a glow halo, never sparks, never a
  second flame.
- Character comes from the slow, ceremonial proportions, never from added
  parts.
- Never add: a second lantern, a face on the lantern, eyes on the glass,
  hats, scarves, a mouth, brows, more than one flame.

## Prompt spec

> the recurring mascot — a tall, thin, spindly creature built like delicate
> wrought-iron garden furniture, with long fine dark forged-metal legs, a
> narrow dark-metal torso and thin metal arms, two tiny dark dot eyes set
> high on the body, blank deadpan (no mouth, no brows), and a small glass
> lantern set into its chest holding ONE small flame; the flame is the ONLY
> accent-colored part. It MUST perform the move — tending and lighting the
> lights — not merely pose. {value rule: the forged-metal body and glass keep
> their dark, muted vintage values; the dot eyes use the structure ink, never
> pure black; the chest flame stays the accent hue in every palette}

## Value rules

- **Dark/dusk palettes** (its native register): the metal body reads in deep
  muted values and the chest flame is the brightest, warmest point in the
  frame — the light it is keeping lit.
- **Light/warm palettes**: the metal body holds mid-to-dark structure-ink
  values (not pure black) with structure-ink eyes; the flame stays the
  accent hue. Exactly one accent, always the flame.

## Personality

Slow, formal, a little ceremonial — tends the flame and keeps the dark at
bay without ever making a show of it. Good for keeping watch, lighting the
way, and warmth through a long night — and for on-call and uptime.
