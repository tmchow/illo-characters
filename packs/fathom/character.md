# Fathom — character pack

A brick-built deep-sea diver in a brass helmet, calmly working the dark floor
of the deep. The name reads off the work — to *fathom* is to measure the
depths and to finally understand something; this little diver goes all the way
down and brings the answer back up.

Credit: **Fathom by Trevin Chow**

Style: **bricks**
Cutout chroma: **magenta**
Aliases: diver, deep-sea-diver

## Locked design

- **Body**: a small, blocky toy-brick minifigure built from glossy ABS-plastic
  bricks — stubby brick arms and legs, simple curved mitten hands, chunky brick
  boots, a cream-and-navy diving suit. Everything it stands in is built from
  flat, studded bricks too; the figure is the only smooth-helmeted thing.
- **Helmet**: a round, bolted, polished-**brass** diving helmet with a single
  circular front porthole — smooth and rounded, one of the few non-blocky
  pieces.
- **Face**: two small round dark dot eyes and a tiny simple smile, seen through
  the porthole glass — blank and deadpan, **no brows**, ever.
- **Accent carrier**: the polished **brass helmet** is the only accent-colored
  part — warm gold against the muted cream-and-navy suit. Never a second metal
  piece, never a glow, never sparks.
- Character comes from the calm, methodical descent, never from added gear.
- Never add: a speargun, a second air tank, swim fins, a mouth beyond the tiny
  smile, brows, a second accent color on the body.

## Value rules

- **Deep/dark palettes** (its native register): the suit and brick terrain read
  in muted, cool plastic values and the brass helmet is the warmest, brightest
  point in the frame — the thing the eye lands on.
- **Light/bright palettes**: the suit holds mid plastic values with
  structure-ink eyes; the brass helmet stays the one accent. Exactly one
  accent, always the brass.

## Personality

Methodical, unbothered, quietly thorough — goes all the way to the bottom,
finds the one thing, and brings it back up without fuss. Good for going deep,
getting to the bottom of things, and the patient work of recovery — and, yes,
deep dives and debugging.
