# Brew — character pack

A squat coffee mug. The name is the process — good work steeps before it
ships, and there's always a build brewing.

Credit: **Brew by Trevin Chow**.

Style: **gouache**

## Locked design

- **Body**: one squat round mug standing on small stubby legs — a single
  soft silhouette, painted as flat matte gouache shapes with no drawn
  outlines.
- **Face**: two simple dot eyes on the mug body, left as bare paper.
  Completely blank, deadpan, neutral — **no eyebrows, no mouth, ever**.
- **Handle**: one loop handle on its left side — the **accent carrier** and
  only accent-colored part. One small stubby arm on the other side.
- Cuteness comes from proportion and roundness, never from added parts.
- Never add: steam, visible liquid, a saucer, patterns or logos on the mug,
  a spoon, eyebrows, mouth, clothing, accessories.

## Value rules

Gouache separates shapes by value, not line (see the style's palette
mapping):

- **Default**: the body is the **paint ink** — the structure ink softened to
  a paint near-black — with the dot eyes left as bare paper.
- On grounds too dark for that to read, a pale body in a tint of the paper
  with paint-ink eyes, separated by value or a thin painted contour — that
  contour is the only line allowed.
- The handle takes the palette accent, shifted slightly matte/desaturated
  per the style's mapping.

## Personality

The patient deep worker — stands under the slow drip and lets it steep, no
fuss, no steam. The character for focus, mornings, breaks, productivity,
percolating ideas, and builds brewing.
