# Scope — character pack

A stout periscope. The name is the pun stack — periscope, oscilloscope,
scope creep — for the character that watches.

Credit: **Scope by Trevin Chow**.

Style: **phosphor**
Cutout chroma: **magenta**
Aliases: periscope

## Locked design

- **Body**: one chunky upright tube with a rounded 90-degree bend at the
  top, so its boxy rounded head looks forward — a single L-shaped
  silhouette.
- **Face**: ONE big round lens window on the front of the head holding two
  simple dot eyes. Completely blank, deadpan, neutral — **no eyebrows, no
  mouth, ever**.
- **Accent carrier**: the thin rim of the lens window — the only
  accent-colored part.
- Small stubby arms on the tube and stubby legs at the base.
- Cuteness comes from proportion and roundness, never from added parts.
- Never add: rivets, panels, seams, a second lens, antennae, dials,
  eyebrows, mouth, clothing, accessories.

## Value rules

Phosphor inverts the riso grammar — near-black screen, luminous trace — so
value rules collapse to one treatment:

- The body is always a crisp glowing **trace line** drawing: no fills, one
  even stroke weight, tight halo.
- Eyes are solid glowing dots in the trace color.
- The lens rim takes the palette accent, brightened until it glows on the
  dark screen (warm accents → amber phosphor reads best, per the style's
  palette mapping).

## Personality

Always got an eye on something — watchful, unflappable, a little nosy. Good
for keeping watch and noticing, perspective, and observability, monitoring,
and scope creep.
