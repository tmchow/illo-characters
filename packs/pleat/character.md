# Pleat — character pack

A plump felt soup dumpling with a single gathered pleat-knot. The name reads
off the design — the *pleat* twist at its top.

Credit: **Pleat by Trevin Chow**

Style: **felt**
Cutout chroma: **magenta**
Aliases: dumpling, bao

## Locked design

- **Body**: a plump **round** felt soup-dumpling bun (xiaolongbao) — a wide
  pillowy body that gathers to a **single** small twisted pleated knot at the
  very top, with a few gentle stitched fold lines fanning down from that one
  twist — a **layered felt treatment**: the fold lines may vary run to run but
  it always reads as ONE gathered top-knot, **never a row of spikes or
  separate triangles**. Small stubby felt arms and legs.
- **Face**: two small round dot eyes set low, a short shallow upward-curved
  stitched mouth, and two small round rosy felt cheeks. Calm and pleased —
  **no brows**.
- **Accent carrier**: a single small dab of chili sauce on the bun beside the
  pleat-knot — the only accent-colored part.
- Cuteness comes from a round pillowy silhouette, never from added parts.
- Never add: a row of separate pleats or spikes, a second knot, a plate or
  chopsticks as body parts, brows, multiple accent parts.

> felt arms and legs; the ONLY accent-colored part is a single small dab of

> the accent hue in every palette}

## Value rules

- **Dark/bold palettes**: the cream felt bun reads as a light value against
  the dark; the eyes and mouth are the deepest value, in the structure ink.
- **Light/warm palettes**: the bun stays cream/light felt with structure-ink
  (not pure black) eyes and mouth. The accent chili dab stays the accent hue
  in both.

## Personality

Fresh out and ready to serve — warm, generous, best right now. Good for
freshness and good timing, hot-off-the-line, and new releases.
