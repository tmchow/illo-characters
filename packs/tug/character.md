# Tug — character pack

A glossy red horseshoe magnet that cannot stop attracting things. The name
is the verb — tug is the pull you feel when one small install starts
dragging everything else along.

Credit: **Tug by Trevin Chow**.

Style: **enamel**
Aliases: magnet, horseshoe-magnet

## Locked design

- **Body**: one thick cast-iron bar bent into a single bold U, standing
  upright as an arch — the bend at the top, uniform thickness end to end,
  no taper, no thin parts. The whole U is ONE deep glossy signal-red enamel
  cell bounded by the raised metal line.
- **Face**: two simple dot eyes on the outside of the bend, high on the
  arch, as small pale enamel cells. Completely blank, deadpan, neutral —
  **no eyebrows, no mouth, ever**.
- **Legs**: the U's two straight ends point down and ARE its stubby legs;
  it stands on them. **No other limbs — no arms, ever**: tug acts on the
  world by attraction and by leaning its whole body into the pull.
- **Accent carrier**: the two **bare-steel tips** — one pale silver-steel
  band capping each end — the classic silver contact ends on red enamel,
  the only accent cells, doubling as its feet and its power source. They
  must visibly contrast with the body red.
- Cuteness comes from proportion and the thickness of the bend, never from
  added parts.
- Never add: N/S letters or any text on the body, arms or hands, a keeper
  bar or handle, magnetic field lines, sparks, lightning bolts, rivets,
  seams, taper, a second bend, extra prongs, eyebrows, mouth, pin-back
  hardware.

## Value rules

Enamel maps value to cell fills (see the style's palette mapping):

- **Metal**: all outlines follow the palette's structure-ink metal mapping
  (near-black structure → black-nickel `#2c2c30`; warm structure → antique
  gold `#b8923f`). One metal color per image; the classic default uses
  antique gold.
- **Body**: the U is always tug's signature deep glossy signal-red enamel
  (`#c1272d` in the classic default) — red IS the magnet, a fixed identity
  material like mole's tan paws. Never recolor the body with the palette
  accent or the structure hue.
- **Eyes**: pale enamel dots in the paper hue (`#f2ead8` classic), each a
  closed metal-bounded cell — the deep body takes pale eyes, per the
  style's character treatment.
- **Accent carrier — the tips**: pale silver-steel enamel (`#dfe3e8`
  classic), the brightest cells on the figure; force the contrast in the
  prompt ("pale cool silver, must NOT be red"). Hardware stuck to tug in
  scenes is the same steel — what it attracts wears its accent.
- **Scene accent**: the palette's accent enamel (`#e0356f` classic, vivid
  magenta-pink) goes to at most one scene cell — usually the single thing
  tug actually wanted.

## Personality

Friendly and a little too magnetic — whatever it touches, the whole pile
comes along. Good for pulling things together, attraction, and dependencies
and lockfiles.
