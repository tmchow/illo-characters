# Prompt — character pack

A little command-line terminal, waiting for you to tell it what to do. The
name reads off the design — it *is* a prompt, blinking caret and all.

Credit: **Prompt by Jai Toor**.

Style: **blueprint**
Cutout chroma: **magenta**
Aliases: terminal, cli, command-line

## Locked design

- **Body**: a chunky rounded terminal-window box — softly rounded corners, a
  thin title-bar strip across the top with three small dots at the left. One
  simple boxy silhouette, nothing else on the shell.
- **Face**: two simple dot eyes and one thin flat straight mouth on the front
  of the box — blank and deadpan, no eyebrows, ever.
- **Accent carrier**: ONE small solid block-caret sitting just to the right of
  the mouth — the only accent-colored part. It is a plain filled rectangle
  (the blinking cursor), never a letter, never text, never a second caret.
  Nothing extends from it: no beam, trail, underline, or horizontal rule.
- Small stubby arms and legs.
- Cuteness comes from proportion and roundness, never from added parts.

## Prompt spec

> the recurring mascot — a chunky rounded terminal-window box body with a thin
> top title-bar and three small dots at its left, two simple dot eyes, one
> thin flat straight mouth, blank deadpan (no eyebrows), small stubby arms and
> legs, and ONE small solid block-caret just to the right of the mouth; the
> block-caret is the ONLY accent-colored part. It stays a compact filled
> rectangle; nothing extends from it — no beam, trail, underline, or horizontal
> rule. It MUST perform the move, not decorate. It is drawn in the same
> construction line as everything else, eyes as solid dots, the block-caret in
> the accent color.

## Value rules

- **Blueprint (native)**: all-line rendering on the deep indigo ground; the
  box, title-bar, and limbs are white construction line; eyes are solid white
  dots; only the block-caret takes the accent.
- If a variant pack ever moves it to a light-paper look: light box body with
  the structure-ink outline, structure-ink eyes, block-caret stays accent.

## Personality

The patient one that waits for the word and then just runs it — deadpan,
literal, quietly capable. Give it the instruction and it does exactly that,
no fuss. Good for getting started, running the thing, turning a plain
sentence into work done, and the small satisfaction of a command that just
works.
