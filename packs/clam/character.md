# Clam — character pack

A smooth gouache clam — a closed bivalve shell on two stub feet. The name is
the behavior: it clams up, showing the world one clean seam while everything
busy stays inside.

Credit: **Clam by Trevin Chow**.

Style: **gouache**
Aliases: shellfish, mollusk

## Locked design

- **Body**: one smooth rounded wedge — a closed bivalve shell, a fan shape
  read as a single soft mass, with exactly **one thin straight seam line**
  across the middle where the two valves meet. The shell is completely
  smooth: no ribs, no flutes, no growth rings, no scallop edge.
- **Face**: two simple dot eyes on the upper shell. Completely blank,
  deadpan, neutral — **no eyebrows, no mouth, ever**. The seam is the
  shell's joint, never an expression: it stays thin, straight, and closed —
  never curved into a smile, never opened.
- **Feet**: two small stub feet below the shell. **No arms** — the clam
  performs with its feet and its body weight.
- **Accent carrier**: the small round **hinge knob at the back of the
  shell** — the only accent-colored part. In front views it peeks above the
  top rear edge of the shell as a single small bump at the crown of the
  silhouette.
- Cuteness comes from proportion and roundness, never from added parts.
- Never add: a pearl (ever), a mouth/smile/teeth/tongue, an open shell used
  as a face, ribs or fluted scallop ridges, growth-ring texture, extra seam
  lines, arms, barnacles, seaweed, bubbles, water drops, gloss or
  pearlescent sheen, eyebrows, hats, clothing, accessories, shiny/anime
  eyes.

## Value rules

Gouache maps value to flat matte paint shapes (see the style's palette
mapping):

- Clam is **light-bodied**: the shell is a pale tint of the paper, one flat
  tone per valve, separated from the ground by value — or by a thin painted
  contour only where contrast fails.
- Eyes are **paint-ink** dots; the seam is one thin **paint-ink** line —
  the only line allowed on the body.
- The stub feet take the **mid** tone (secondary shapes), never the accent.
- The hinge knob takes the **palette accent**, shifted slightly matte and
  desaturated. Always name the accent hue in words beside the hex — accent
  colors drift otherwise (classic default: tomato red-orange `#d95f3b`).
- Matte everywhere: no gradients, no blended shading, no gloss — a shiny or
  pearlescent shell is off-model.

## Personality

The interface — shows the world one seam and nothing else. Unbothered by
whatever is erupting inside; the lid stays down and the surface stays
simple. The character for encapsulation and clean interfaces: API design,
abstraction boundaries, information hiding, "simple outside, busy inside" —
boss guards against attackers; clam decides what gets exposed, by design.
