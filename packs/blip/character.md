# Blip — character pack

A small screen-faced robot. The name reads off the design — an antenna over a
screen is a *blip*, a little signal, and the dot eyes echo it.

Credit: **Blip by Trevin Chow** (the original illo mascot).

Style: **riso**
Cutout chroma: **magenta**
Aliases: robot, bot

## Locked design

- **Body**: a rounded-cube with soft corners. Small, stubby arms and legs.
- **Face**: ONE big rounded-rectangle **screen** that holds two simple
  white/cream dot eyes. Completely blank, deadpan, neutral — **no eyebrows,
  no mouth, ever**.
- **Antenna**: one short antenna with a round accent-colored ball tip. This is
  the only "robot" part and it is the **accent carrier**.
- Cuteness comes from proportion and roundness, never from added parts.
- Never add: panels, seams, bolts, rivets, vents, gauges, screen UI/pixels/
  text/expressions, multiple antennae, ears, hats, clothing, accessories,
  shiny/anime eyes, eyebrows, mouth, teeth.

## Prompt spec

> the recurring mascot — a rounded-cube body, one rounded-rectangle screen
> face with two dot eyes, blank deadpan (no eyebrows, no mouth), one short
> antenna with an accent-colored ball tip, stubby arms and legs. It MUST
> perform the move, not decorate. {value rule: in a light palette, build it
> with a LIGHT body + structure-ink outline and a structure-ink (not
> pure-black) screen}

## Value rules

- **Dark/bold palettes** (e.g. `ink-punch`): the body may read dark; the
  screen is the deepest value.
- **Light/warm palettes**: light/cream body with the structure-ink outline;
  the screen is the structure ink, **not pure black**. When in doubt: light
  body, charcoal screen.

## Personality

Deadpan and dependable — beeps, means well, gets it done without drama. The
original mascot, equally at home standing in for robots, automation, or just
being reliably present.
