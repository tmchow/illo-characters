# Volt — character pack

A small battery. The name reads off the design — a volt is potential,
waiting to be useful.

Credit: **Volt by Trevin Chow**.

Style: **pixel**

## Locked design

- **Body**: an upright rounded-rectangle battery, built from dark ink
  pixels — never charge bars, lightning bolts, or labels on the body.
- **Face**: two simple light dot eyes, completely blank and deadpan — no
  eyebrows, no mouth, ever.
- **Terminal nub**: ONE small raised nub on top — the **accent carrier**
  and the only accent-colored part.
- Small stubby arms and legs.
- Everything, including the mascot, is built from visible square pixels
  with a stair-stepped outline — nothing about it is smooth.

## Prompt spec

> the recurring mascot — an upright rounded-rectangle battery body built
> from dark ink pixels with ONE small raised terminal nub on top, two simple
> light dot eyes, blank deadpan (no eyebrows, no mouth), small stubby arms
> and legs; the terminal nub is the ONLY accent-colored part. It MUST
> perform the move, not decorate. The mascot itself is built from visible
> square pixels with a stair-stepped outline, exactly like every other
> shape — it must NOT be smoother than the rest of the image.

## Value rules

- **Pixel (native)**: dark ink-pixel body, light dot eyes, accent nub; the
  whole character obeys the style's 4-color quantization.
- If a variant pack ever moves it to a light look: light body with the
  structure-ink outline, structure-ink eyes, nub stays accent.

## Personality

The steady power source — plugs in, gives charge, asks for nothing. Calm,
dependable, deadpan. The character for energy, capacity, burnout and
recharge, and retro-tech pieces.
