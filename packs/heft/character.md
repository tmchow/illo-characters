# Heft — character pack

A stout ball-rolling beetle that pushes loads many times its size. The name
is the weight — heft is what it puts its shoulder into.

Credit: **Heft by Trevin Chow**.

Style: **woodcut**
Aliases: beetle, dung-beetle, scarab

## Locked design

- **Body**: one domed wing-case — a single solid half-circle dome that
  dominates the outline — over a small tucked head at the front rim. One
  unbroken silhouette; it reads as a determined half-circle at any size.
- **Face**: two simple dot eyes on the tucked head, left as uncarved paper.
  Completely blank, deadpan, neutral — **no eyebrows, no mouth, ever**.
- **Legs**: exactly **four** stubby legs beneath the dome — never six. The
  two front feet end in flat pusher pads; the back pair are plain stubs.
- **Accent carrier**: the matched pair of flat pusher pads on the two front
  feet — one working pair, the only accent-colored part.
- Cuteness comes from proportion and roundness, never from added parts.
- Never add: a fifth or sixth leg, antennae, a wing-case split seam or
  elytra line, body segments, a horn, mandibles or any mouth parts, claws,
  toes, spots or pips on the shell, folk-paint decoration, wings, teeth,
  tools, hats, clothing, accessories, eyebrows, shiny/anime eyes.

## Prompt spec

> the recurring mascot — a stout ball-rolling beetle: one solid domed
> wing-case (a single unbroken half-circle dome, no split seam, no
> segments) over a small tucked head, two simple dot eyes, blank deadpan
> (no eyebrows, no mouth, no antennae, no mandibles), exactly four stubby
> legs beneath; the two front feet end in flat pusher pads, and that
> matched pair of pads is the ONLY accent-colored part (state the accent
> hue in words next to the hex — accent inks drift otherwise). It MUST
> perform the move — shoulder down, pads planted, shoving or rolling the
> load — not decorate. {value rule: the dome and body are carved solid in
> the carving ink with coarse gouge marks in the fill, the eyes are
> uncarved paper dots, and only the pusher pads print in the accent ink,
> slightly off-register}

## Value rules

Woodcut allows only three values — paper, ink, accent (no midtones):

- The dome and body are carved **solid in the carving ink** (the structure
  ink deepened to near-black while keeping its temperature), with coarse
  parallel gouge lines visible in the big dome fill.
- The eyes are **uncarved paper dots** — the paper color showing through
  the ink, never white paint.
- The pusher pads take the **palette accent ink**, printed slightly
  off-register — vermilion red-orange `#d8401f` in the classic palette.
  Always name the accent hue in words beside the hex; it drifts otherwise.
- Nothing else takes the accent: head, legs, and dome stay carving ink, and
  the slight off-register misprint on the pads is correct — don't fix it.

## Personality

Assumes everything is liftable, eventually. The character for tech debt and
heavy lifting: legacy weight, the big slow refactor, rolling the boulder
uphill, grind work that moves something enormous a little every day. Pip
carries small parcels for others; Heft moves loads bigger than itself, one
shove at a time, with no opinion about how long it takes.
