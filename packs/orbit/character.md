# Orbit — character pack

A brick-built astronaut in a white suit and amber visor, happiest one step past
where the map ends. The name reads off the job — once you've left the pad,
everything is about reaching *orbit*: the launch, the leap, the first look back
at the whole world.

Credit: **Orbit by Trevin Chow**

Style: **bricks**
Cutout chroma: **magenta**
Aliases: astronaut, spaceman

## Locked design

- **Body**: a small blocky toy-brick minifigure in a glossy **white** ABS-plastic
  spacesuit — stubby white brick limbs, simple curved mitten hands, chunky
  boots, a small printed control panel on the chest, and a boxy oxygen-tank
  backpack. The world around it is built from flat, studded bricks.
- **Helmet**: a smooth white domed helmet with a curved, translucent **amber
  visor** — one of the few non-blocky, rounded pieces.
- **Face**: two small round dark dot eyes and a tiny simple smile behind the
  visor — blank and deadpan, **no brows**.
- **Accent carrier**: the translucent **amber visor** is the only
  accent-colored part — a warm glow against the clean white suit. Never a
  second colored light, never a second visor, never a jetpack flame.
- Character comes from the eager, exploring posture, never from added equipment.
- Never add: a second tank beyond the one backpack, a colored suit, a mouth
  beyond the tiny smile, brows, a second accent.

## Prompt spec

> the recurring mascot — a small blocky toy-brick astronaut in a glossy white
> ABS-plastic spacesuit with stubby white brick limbs, simple curved mitten
> hands, a small printed chest control panel and a boxy oxygen-tank backpack,
> and a smooth white domed helmet with a curved translucent amber visor; behind
> the visor are two dark dot eyes and a tiny smile, blank and deadpan; the amber
> visor is the ONLY accent-colored part. The whole scene is built from flat,
> studded toy bricks. It MUST perform the move — launching, planting the flag,
> reaching past the edge — not merely pose. {value rule: the white suit and
> brick world hold clean neutral plastic values; the dot eyes use the structure
> ink, never pure black; the amber visor stays the warm accent in every palette}

## Value rules

- **Dark/space palettes** (its native register): the white suit reads bright
  against deep black brick space and the amber visor is the warmest point in the
  frame.
- **Light/bright palettes**: the white suit holds soft neutral plastic values
  with structure-ink eyes; the amber visor stays the one accent. Exactly one
  accent, always the visor.

## Personality

Eager, unflappable, a little wide-eyed — already halfway to the next thing,
flag in hand. Good for launches, exploring the unknown, and the leap past where
the map ends — and, yes, shipping and the go-for-launch call.
