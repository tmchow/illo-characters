# Lapse — character pack

An hourglass. The name is what it measures — the time that slips while the
task is still open.

Credit: **Lapse by Trevin Chow**.

Style: **chalk**
Cutout chroma: **magenta**
Aliases: hourglass, sandglass, egg-timer

## Locked design

- **Body**: an hourglass — two rounded glass bulbs pinched at a narrow
  waist, one simple symmetric silhouette. A small pool of sand rests in the
  lower bulb.
- **Face**: two simple dot eyes on the upper bulb. Completely blank,
  deadpan, neutral — **no eyebrows, no mouth, ever**.
- **Accent carrier**: the sand — the only accent-colored part, shaded with
  the side of the accent chalk.
- Small stubby arms at the waist and stubby legs under the base.
- Cuteness comes from proportion and roundness, never from added parts.
- Never add: ornate frame posts, a falling-sand stream, cracks, numerals or
  tick marks, eyebrows, mouth, clothing, accessories.

## Prompt spec

> the recurring mascot — an hourglass with two rounded glass bulbs pinched
> at a narrow waist and a small pool of sand in the lower bulb, two simple
> dot eyes on the upper bulb, blank deadpan (no eyebrows, no mouth), small
> stubby arms at the waist and stubby legs under the base; the ONLY
> accent-colored part is the sand. It MUST perform the move, not decorate.
> Drawn in the same confident chalk stroke as everything else, eyes as solid
> chalk dots, the sand shaded in accent chalk. {value rule: chalk line
> drawing on the slate — no fills}

## Value rules

Chalk inverts the riso grammar — dark board, light line — so value rules
collapse to one treatment:

- The body is always a chalk **line drawing** on the slate: no fills, glass
  rendered as outline only.
- Eyes are solid dots in the chalk color.
- The sand pool takes the palette accent as colored chalk, lifted/dusted
  until it reads on the dark board (a dark accent rotates toward a warm
  pastel, per the style's palette mapping).

## Personality

Watches the sand go with serene resignation. Good for the passage of time,
patience, deadlines and timeboxes, and noticing where the time went.
