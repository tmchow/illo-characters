# Coil — character pack

A small, round-faced retro tin-toy robot, the classic 1950s archetype. The
name reads off the design — its arms and legs are bendy, ribbed accordion-hose
*coils*, the most identifiable thing about it.

Credit: **Coil by Trevin Chow**.

Style: **sketchbook**
Cutout chroma: **magenta**
Aliases: tin-robot, tin-toy

## Locked design

- **Head**: a rounded-square / boxy head. ONE thin antenna rising from the top,
  topped by a small round ball. A round disc "ear" on each side of the head.
- **Face**: two large round cartoon eyes with visible dark pupils (expressive,
  this is a warm emotive robot — not a deadpan one), and one simple thin mouth.
  Brow and mouth *shape* carry the emotion; the parts themselves never change.
- **Body**: a square torso panel with a smaller inset rectangular plate on the
  chest. Riveted seams and small bolt details at the joints.
- **Limbs**: the signature — arms and legs are segmented, ribbed, bendy
  accordion-hose / vacuum-cord tubes, each ONE continuous coil that can curl
  and pose loosely. Always one tube per limb, never a tangle.
- **Hands**: simple three-fingered claw/mitt hands.
- **Antenna ball tip**: the **accent carrier** — the one part that may glow the
  rare cool teal-green signal color. Everything else on the robot stays warm
  sepia.
- Never add: a second antenna, treads/wheels, a screen-face, extra eyes,
  clothing, a chest gauge/dial, jointed rigid (non-ribbed) limbs, or solid
  block hands. Smooth straight rods instead of ribbed coils = wrong robot.

## Prompt spec

> the recurring mascot — Coil, a small round-faced 1950s tin-toy robot: a
> rounded-square head with one thin ball-tipped antenna and a round disc ear on
> each side, two large round cartoon eyes with dark pupils and one simple thin
> mouth (warm and expressive — emotion via brow and mouth shape only), a square
> torso with a small inset chest plate and riveted bolt seams, and its
> signature segmented ribbed bendy accordion-hose arms and legs (one continuous
> coil per limb) ending in three-fingered claw hands. It MUST perform the move,
> not pose. {value rule: drawn in monochrome warm sepia like the whole scene;
> the only accent is a tiny cool teal-green glow at the antenna ball tip}

## Value rules

- **The robot is monochrome warm sepia**, built from the same cross-hatched
  pencil-and-ink as the scene — light cream tin body with warm brown-black ink
  lines and hatched shading on the panels.
- **Exactly one cool accent**: the antenna ball tip may glow a small, sparing
  teal-green. No other part of the robot carries color. Scene-level cool pops
  (a teal CRT, a pale-blue teardrop) are allowed but stay tiny and rare.
- In any palette, keep the body light cream with sepia ink; the deepest values
  are the hatched recesses and cast shadows, never a flat black fill.

## Personality

An eager, well-meaning everyrobot — endearing and genuinely emotive. It throws
itself into the work and feels it: delighted and curious at the help desk,
gleefully overwhelmed juggling a dozen tasks, crestfallen (a single cartoon
teardrop) over a rough review. Round, warm, and trying its best — the friendly
face for retro-tech, automation, and the small daily dramas of working life.
