# Sulk — character pack

A dumpy grey city pigeon that has seen it all and is not impressed.

Credit: **Sulk by Trevin Chow**

Style: **bloom**
Cutout chroma: **magenta**
Aliases: pigeon, dove

## Locked design

- **Body**: a plump, round city pigeon — a low, dumpy oval body, a small head
  set close to the shoulders, and short stubby legs with small feet. Stout and
  earthbound, never sleek.
- **Face**: two small dot eyes under heavy, half-lidded lids — a permanently
  unimpressed, deadpan stare. No brows, no mouth beyond the beak.
- **Accent carrier**: the short triangular **beak** — the only accent-colored
  part.
- Cuteness and humor come from the round dumpy silhouette and the flat stare,
  never from added parts.
- Never add: a second accent part, neck iridescence patterns, a mouth, brows,
  or clothing.

## Prompt spec

> the recurring mascot — a plump, round city pigeon with a low dumpy oval body,
> a small head set close to the shoulders, and short stubby legs; two small dot
> eyes under heavy half-lidded lids giving a permanently unimpressed deadpan
> stare; the ONLY accent-colored part is the short triangular beak. It MUST
> perform the move, not decorate. The body is a flat solid grey cel shape with
> ONE bold, softly-rounded outline; no texture or shading on the body beyond at
> most one soft rim of light along the lit edge.

## Value rules

- **Dark/bold palettes**: the body reads as a mid-grey against the dim ground,
  separated by its bold outline; eyes are the deepest value.
- **Light palettes**: the body stays a clean flat grey with a strong outline so
  it still reads as a pigeon. The beak stays the accent hue in every palette.

## Personality

Perpetually unimpressed and entirely unbothered — the pigeon that holds its
ground on the windowsill while the world hurries past. Good for deadpan
reactions, waiting, and the things that just keep showing up.
