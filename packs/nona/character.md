# Nona — character pack

A plain wooden nesting doll. The name reads off the design — nonna the
grandmother, Nona the Fate who spins the thread: every release she ships,
the previous version still lives inside her. She IS the changelog.

Credit: **Nona by Trevin Chow**.

Style: **woodcut**
Aliases: nesting-doll, matryoshka, russian-doll, doll

## Locked design

- **Body**: one continuous egg-shaped nesting-doll mass — wide rounded
  bottom, smaller rounded top, flat base, **no neck break** — a single
  unbroken silhouette of plain unpainted wood.
- **Seam**: a single horizontal seam at the midline where she opens, drawn
  as ONE clean painted band. This is the accent carrier.
- **Face**: two simple dot eyes directly on the upper body, blank deadpan —
  **no eyebrows, no mouth, ever**.
- **Limbs**: small stubby arms for lifting and tiny stub feet under the
  flat base — no hands, no fingers spelled out.
- **Accent carrier**: the seam band — the only accent-colored part.
- Cuteness comes from proportion and roundness, never from added parts.
- Never add: folk painting, floral patterns, a headscarf or shawl outline,
  a painted face panel or rosy cheeks, decorative borders, extra seams or
  bands, a neck break or collar, halftone dots, hats, clothing,
  accessories, eyebrows, mouth, shiny/anime eyes.

## Value rules

Woodcut allows no midtones — every area is paper, carving ink, or accent
(see the style's palette mapping):

- The body is **bare paper** (warm cream `#f6efe0`) with a thick carved
  near-black outline (`#161311`) — a light-bodied character per the
  style's character treatment. Texture in large fills comes from coarse
  parallel gouge marks, never halftone dots or gray midtones.
- Eyes are carving-ink dots; arms and feet are outlined paper like the
  body.
- The seam band prints in the **accent ink** (classic vermilion
  `#d8401f`), slightly off-register — that misregistration is correct,
  don't fix it. Beyond the seam, at most 1–2 accent elements per scene.

## Personality

Carries every version of herself inside, the newest holding all the rest.
Good for history and layers, honoring what came before, and releases and
versioning.
