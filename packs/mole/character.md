# Mole — character pack

A plump clay mole. The name is the job — the thing that digs past the
surface until it reaches the root.

Credit: **Mole by Trevin Chow**.

Style: **clay**

## Locked design

- **Body**: one plump teardrop mass with a small pointed snout — a single
  soft silhouette, hand-modeled in matte clay.
- **Face**: two simple clay-bead dot eyes. Completely blank, deadpan,
  neutral — **no eyebrows, no mouth, ever**.
- **Paws**: two big flat mitt-shaped digging paws as arms (soft mitts, no
  claws, no fingers spelled out) and small stubby legs, in a muted warm tan
  clay — secondary clay, never accent.
- **Accent carrier**: the round tip of its nose — the only accent-colored
  part.
- Cuteness comes from proportion and roundness, never from added parts.
- Never add: whiskers, fur texture, claws, teeth, tools, hats, clothing,
  accessories, eyebrows, mouth, shiny/anime eyes.

## Prompt spec

> in the set — never a flat drawing. {value rule: body in the main clay
> mapped from the structure ink, eyes as set-colored beads}

## Value rules

Clay maps value to material color (see the style's palette mapping):

- The body is always the **main clay** — the structure ink lifted to a clay
  material color, keeping its temperature (near-black structure → warm
  charcoal clay).
- Eyes are beads in the **set color** (the mapped paper); paws and feet stay
  muted warm tan, secondary in every palette.
- The nose tip takes the palette accent as matte accent clay. Always name
  the accent hue in words beside the hex — clay renders drift accent color
  toward toy defaults (observed: coral → yellow).

## Personality

The methodical digger — unhurried, thorough, already three layers down while
everyone else is staring at the wilted plant. The character for debugging,
root-cause analysis, investigation, going deep, and excavating legacy code.
