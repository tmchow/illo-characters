# Patch — character pack

A fat roll of tape standing on edge. The name is the job — the honest
temporary fix that stretches itself over the crack and holds until the real
one ships.

Credit: **Patch by Trevin Chow**.

Style: **manila**

## Locked design

- **Body**: one fat roll of tape — a soft thick ring standing upright on its
  edge, with a big readable hole through the middle. The hole is real
  negative space: the ground always shows through it, in every render.
- **Face**: two simple dot eyes directly on the rim, blank deadpan — **no
  eyebrows, no mouth, ever**.
- **Limbs**: small stubby arms at the sides of the ring and small stubby
  legs below — enough to plant its feet and press a seam flat, no hands or
  detail.
- **Accent carrier**: the lifted, folded-over tab where the tape ends — one
  small flag-like fold rising off the rim, the only accent-colored part.
- Cuteness comes from proportion and roundness, never from added parts.
- Never add: a filled-in center (the hole stays open), inner spiral or
  concentric layer lines, serrated or zigzag tape edges, a dispenser or
  cutter blade, printed text or stripes on the tape, extra tabs or trailing
  tape ends, teeth, eyebrows, mouth, hats, clothing, accessories.

## Value rules

Manila maps value to stampings (see the style's palette mapping):

- The body is **light-bodied**: bare manila with one chunky stamped
  structure-ink outline — masking tape is paper-colored, so the roll is
  built exactly like every other stamped prop. Eyes are stamp-ink dots.
- The center hole is **ground, not fill** — bare manila showing straight
  through the ring. No palette ever fills it.
- The tab takes the palette accent as the "second stamp" — classic stamp
  red `#c8372d` in the default manila palette — and may sit a few degrees
  off-angle like a separate hand stamping. Always name the accent hue in
  words beside the hex; accent colors drift otherwise.
- In a dark palette the ring stamps solid structure ink with paper-light
  eyes; the hole still reads open.

## Personality

Knows it's temporary; sticks anyway. Patch never claims to be the real fix
— it files itself under "stopgap", plants its feet, and holds the seam
shut for as long as it takes, which is usually forever. The character for
hotfixes, workarounds, duct-tape engineering, and everything that ships
with a TODO attached.
