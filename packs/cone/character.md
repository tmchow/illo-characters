# Cone — character pack

A small traffic cone. The name is the design — the most honest piece of
street furniture there is: it stands somewhere inconvenient and won't move.

Credit: **Cone by Trevin Chow**.

Style: **riso**
Aliases: traffic-cone, pylon

## Locked design

- **Body**: a soft-edged traffic cone — gently rounded tip, slightly curved
  sides, one small rounded base. No reflective texture, no second stripe.
- **Face**: two simple dot eyes on the upper cone, completely blank and
  deadpan — no eyebrows, no mouth, no frown, ever.
- **Stripe**: ONE horizontal band around the middle — the **accent carrier**
  and the only accent-colored part.
- Small stubby arms from the sides; stubby little legs under the base.
- Cuteness comes from proportion and roundness, never from added parts.

## Prompt spec

> the recurring mascot — a soft-edged traffic-cone body with a gently rounded
> tip and a small rounded base, two simple dark dot eyes on the upper cone,
> blank deadpan (no eyebrows, no mouth, no frown), ONE single horizontal
> accent stripe around its middle, small stubby arms and legs; the stripe is
> the ONLY accent-colored part. It MUST perform the move, not decorate. The
> body is light paper-colored with the bold structure-ink outline.

## Value rules

- **All palettes**: the body stays light/paper-colored with the structure-ink
  outline; the eyes are structure ink; only the stripe takes the accent.

## Scene guardrail

Cone's reference conditioning likes to clone itself into props — when the
scene contains queues, crowds, or pylons, state explicitly that Cone is the
ONLY character and that the other objects have no faces.

## Personality

The immovable object, deadpan about it. Cone doesn't cause problems — it
*marks* them, by standing exactly where the problem is.
