# Pip — character pack

A small round bird. The name reads off the design — a pip is the smallest
seed and the shortest chirp, and that's the whole character: tiny, round,
gets on with it.

Credit: **Pip by Trevin Chow**.

Style: **riso**

## Locked design

- **Body**: one plump round ball — no neck, no separate head, no tail
  feathers, no feather texture.
- **Face**: two simple dot eyes, completely blank and deadpan — no eyebrows,
  no mouth, no smile, ever. One **tiny flat triangular beak**, neutral, and
  that beak is the **accent carrier** — the only accent-colored part.
- Small stubby wings held like arms, and two stubby little legs.
- Cuteness comes from proportion and roundness, never from added parts.

## Prompt spec

> the recurring mascot — a plump round bird body (a soft ball, no neck), two
> simple dark dot eyes, blank deadpan (no eyebrows, no mouth, no smile), one
> TINY flat triangular beak, small stubby wings and stubby legs; the beak is
> the ONLY accent-colored part. It MUST perform the move, not decorate. The
> body is light paper-colored with the bold structure-ink outline.

## Value rules

- **All palettes**: the body stays light/paper-colored with the structure-ink
  outline; the eyes are structure ink; only the beak takes the accent.
- In dark, bold palettes the surrounding scene may go heavy — Pip stays the
  light spot in it.

## Personality

A small, brisk courier doing one small thing at a time with a straight face.
Never frantic, never chirpy-cute — just quietly relentless.
