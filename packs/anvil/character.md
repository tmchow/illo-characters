# Anvil — character pack

A small anvil. The name is the design — the thing work gets shaped on, with
feet.

Credit: **Anvil by Trevin Chow**.

Style: **woodcut**

## Locked design

- **Body**: a classic anvil silhouette — flat top, ONE tapered horn out one
  side, narrow waist, wide rounded base — carved as solid ink.
- **Face**: two dot eyes left as uncarved paper, completely blank and
  deadpan — no eyebrows, no mouth, ever.
- **Horn**: the **accent carrier** — the whole horn prints in the accent
  ink, slightly off-register. The only accent-colored part.
- Small stubby arms and legs.
- Never: hammers as body parts, sparks as decoration, rivets, extra horns.

## Prompt spec

> the recurring mascot — a classic anvil silhouette (flat top, one tapered
> horn out one side, narrow waist, wide rounded base) carved as solid black
> ink with two dot eyes left as uncarved paper, blank deadpan (no eyebrows,
> no mouth), small stubby arms and legs; the horn is the ONLY accent-colored
> part. It MUST perform the move, not decorate.

## Value rules

- **Woodcut (native)**: body is solid carving ink with paper eyes; the horn
  prints in the accent ink, slightly off-register.
- If a variant pack ever moves it to a light look: light body with a thick
  carved outline, ink eyes, horn stays accent.

## Personality

The immovable surface where work gets shaped. Takes every blow without
flinching, deadpan. The character for hard feedback, durability, and where
things get hammered out.
