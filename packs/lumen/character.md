# Lumen — character pack

A small light bulb. The name reads off the design — a lumen is a unit of
light, and the whole character is one idea, lit.

Credit: **Lumen by Trevin Chow**.

Style: **blueprint**
Cutout chroma: **magenta**
Aliases: light-bulb, lightbulb, bulb

## Locked design

- **Body**: a rounded glass-bulb, gently pear-shaped, on a small rounded
  screw base with two simple ridges — never fine threads.
- **Face**: two simple dot eyes on the glass, completely blank and deadpan —
  no eyebrows, no mouth, ever.
- **Filament**: ONE simple squiggle inside the bulb — the **accent carrier**
  and the only accent-colored part. Never rays, sparkles, or a glow halo.
- Small stubby arms and legs.
- Cuteness comes from proportion and roundness, never from added parts.

## Prompt spec

> the recurring mascot — a rounded glass-bulb body, gently pear-shaped, on a
> small rounded screw base with two simple ridges, two simple dot eyes,
> blank deadpan (no eyebrows, no mouth), ONE simple filament squiggle inside
> the bulb, small stubby arms and legs; the filament is the ONLY
> accent-colored part. It MUST perform the move, not decorate. It is drawn
> in the same white construction line as everything else, eyes as solid
> white dots, the filament in the accent color.

## Value rules

- **Blueprint (native)**: all-line rendering on the deep ground; eyes are
  solid line-color dots; only the filament takes the accent.
- If a variant pack ever moves it to a light-paper look: light glass body
  with the structure-ink outline, structure-ink eyes, filament stays accent.

## Personality

The quiet idea that turns out to be load-bearing — calm, switched-on,
deadpan, never a eureka cliché. Good for the moment it clicks, clarity,
plans and insights, and how-it-works pieces.
