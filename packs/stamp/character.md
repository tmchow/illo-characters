# Stamp — character pack

An upright rubber stamp. The name is the verb — nothing ships until it gets
the stamp.

Credit: **Stamp by Trevin Chow**.

Style: **manila**
Cutout chroma: **magenta**
Aliases: rubber-stamp

## Locked design

- **Body**: one flat rounded-rectangle stamp base standing upright — a
  single chunky silhouette.
- **Face**: two simple dot eyes on the front face of the base, left as bare
  ground color. Completely blank, deadpan, neutral — **no eyebrows, no
  mouth, ever**.
- **Handle**: one short rounded knob handle on top — the **accent carrier**,
  stamped slightly off-angle like a second pressing.
- Small stubby arms and legs.
- Cuteness comes from proportion and roundness, never from added parts.
- Never add: letters or words on the stamp itself, an ink pad, paper
  accessories, handles beyond the one knob, eyebrows, mouth, clothing.

## Prompt spec

> the recurring mascot — an upright rubber stamp: a flat rounded-rectangle
> base standing on small stubby legs, two simple dot eyes on the base's
> front face, blank deadpan (no eyebrows, no mouth), small stubby arms; on
> top one short rounded knob handle — the ONLY accent-colored part, stamped
> slightly off-angle. It MUST perform the move, not decorate. No letters or
> words anywhere on the stamp. {value rule: solid stamped-ink body with
> bare-ground eyes; in a light treatment, ground-colored body with a chunky
> stamped outline and ink eyes}

## Value rules

Manila maps the riso value rules natively:

- **Default (dark-capable)**: the body is stamped solid in the stamp ink;
  the dot eyes stay bare ground (manila).
- **Light treatment**: ground-colored body with a chunky stamped outline,
  ink eyes.
- The knob handle takes the palette accent in both cases, allowed a few
  degrees off-angle — that misregistration is correct, don't fix it.
- Scene papers stay blank: stamps attract type, so any sheet near this
  character must be stated blank (marks are plain filled shapes) with labels
  floated clear in empty ground.

## Personality

Decisive and a little ceremonial — one firm press and it's settled. Good for
making it official, approvals and sign-off, and the decision that finally
lets things move.
