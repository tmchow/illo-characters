# Squall — character pack

A small storm cloud with a lightning-bolt tail. The name is the weather it
brings — the short, violent storm every production system eventually meets.

Credit: **Squall by Trevin Chow**.

Style: **chalk**
Aliases: cloud, storm-cloud, thundercloud, storm

## Locked design

- **Body**: a small puffy cumulus cloud — one flat-bottomed mass with
  exactly three rounded bumps along the top, wider than tall, a single
  closed silhouette.
- **Face**: two simple dot eyes directly on the cloud. Completely blank,
  deadpan, neutral — **no eyebrows, no mouth, ever**.
- **Limbs**: small stubby arms at the sides of the cloud. No legs — it
  hovers, with its tail hanging below.
- **Accent carrier**: a single short zigzag lightning bolt hanging beneath
  the cloud like a tail — the only accent-colored part, shaded with the
  side of the accent chalk. Everything else stays cloud-grey chalk line.
- Cuteness comes from proportion and roundness, never from added parts.
- Never add: raindrops or rain of any kind, a second bolt, mouth or smile,
  eyebrows, blush marks, swirly scribbles inside the cloud, extra bumps,
  wind swooshes, an umbrella, a sun, clothing, accessories.

## Value rules

Chalk inverts the riso grammar — dark board, light line — so value rules
collapse to one treatment:

- The body is always a chalk **line drawing** on the slate: the cloud is
  outline only, no fill, no interior scribble.
- Eyes are solid dots in the chalk color.
- The bolt-tail takes the palette accent as colored chalk, lifted/dusted
  until it reads on the dark board (a dark accent rotates toward a warm
  pastel, per the style's palette mapping). The cloud body never takes the
  accent.

## Personality

The rough patch, reporting for duty — rains on nobody but itself and never
mentions it. Good for the hard days you weather, and for incidents, outages,
war rooms, and 3 a.m. pages.
