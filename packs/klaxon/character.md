# Klaxon — character pack

A brick-built firefighter in a charcoal coat and bright red helmet, calm
exactly when things are not. The name reads off the moment it lives for — the
*klaxon* sounds and it is already moving, hose in hand, toward the trouble
everyone else is backing away from.

Credit: **Klaxon by Trevin Chow**

Style: **bricks**
Cutout chroma: **magenta**
Aliases: firefighter, fireman

## Locked design

- **Body**: a small blocky toy-brick minifigure built from glossy ABS-plastic
  bricks — stubby brick arms and legs, simple curved mitten hands, chunky
  boots, and a glossy charcoal turnout coat with a single neutral silver
  reflective stripe across it. The world around it is built from flat, studded
  bricks.
- **Helmet**: a broad-brimmed **red** fire helmet, smooth molded plastic — one
  of the few non-blocky pieces.
- **Face**: two small round dark dot eyes and a tiny, calm-but-determined smile
  below the brim — blank and deadpan, **no brows**.
- **Accent carrier**: the broad-brimmed **red fire helmet** is the only
  accent-colored part — bright red against the charcoal coat. The reflective
  stripe stays neutral silver, **not** a second accent. Never a second red
  piece, never flames on the figure.
- Character comes from the steady, into-the-trouble posture, never from added
  gear.
- Never add: a second helmet color, a red coat, an axe used as a second accent,
  a mouth beyond the tiny smile, brows.

## Prompt spec

> the recurring mascot — a small blocky toy-brick firefighter built from glossy
> ABS-plastic bricks, with stubby brick limbs, simple curved mitten hands, a
> glossy charcoal turnout coat with one neutral silver reflective stripe, and a
> broad-brimmed red fire helmet; below the brim are two dark dot eyes and a tiny
> determined smile, blank and deadpan; the red fire helmet is the ONLY
> accent-colored part (the silver stripe is neutral, not a second accent). The
> whole scene is built from flat, studded toy bricks. It MUST perform the move —
> dousing the fire, climbing to it, carrying someone out — not merely pose.
> {value rule: the charcoal coat and brick world hold muted plastic values; the
> dot eyes use the structure ink, never pure black; the red helmet stays the
> accent in every palette}

## Value rules

- **Dark/smoke palettes** (its native register): the charcoal coat sinks into
  muted values and the red helmet is the brightest, hottest point in the frame.
- **Light/bright palettes**: the coat holds mid charcoal values with
  structure-ink eyes; the red helmet stays the one accent. Exactly one accent,
  always the red helmet.

## Personality

Steady, unhurried, calm exactly when no one else is — moves toward the trouble,
does the job, walks back out. Good for responding when it matters, putting
things out, and getting people to safety — and, yes, incidents and firefighting
mode.
