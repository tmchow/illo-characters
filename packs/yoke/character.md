# Yoke — character pack

A placid zebu ox — the original automation. The name is the deal: a yoke is
how you hitch power to a task and walk away, and that is exactly what this
character is for.

Credit: **Yoke by Trevin Chow**.

Style: **blueprint**
Cutout chroma: **magenta**
Aliases: ox, zebu, oxen, cattle

## Locked design

- **Body**: one massive rounded block of an ox — a single boulder-like body
  mass with a single bold shoulder hump rising where the load rests. At
  thumbnail size it reads as a boulder with legs.
- **Face**: two simple dot eyes directly on the head mass. Completely blank,
  deadpan, neutral — **no eyebrows, no mouth, no nostrils, ever**.
- **Horns**: two tiny crescent horns, kept stubby — proportion notes, never
  weapons.
- **Legs**: four stubby legs, no hooves spelled out.
- **Accent carrier**: the shoulder hump — the part that takes the load is
  the only accent-colored part.
- Cuteness comes from proportion and roundness, never from added parts.
- Never add: ears, tail, dewlap folds, nostrils, mouth, fur or hide texture,
  muscle definition, nose ring, bell, harness or yoke hardware on the body
  itself, long or sharp horns, gears, panels, robot details, eyebrows,
  shiny/anime eyes.

## Value rules

Blueprint inverts the usual grammar — dark ground, light line (see the
style's palette mapping):

- The body is **pure line work**: the line color (paper lifted to
  near-white, classic `#f4f8ff`) on the deep blueprint ground (structure-ink
  hue deepened, classic `#193a8c`). No fills, no shading — dark-body rules
  collapse to line-on-ground.
- Eyes are **solid dots in the line color**.
- The shoulder hump is **filled with the palette accent**, unchanged from
  the source palette (classic warm orange `#ff7a1a`). If a palette's accent
  is cool and vanishes against the ground, warm it by hue rotation toward
  orange. Always name the accent hue in words beside the hex — accent
  colors drift otherwise.

## Personality

Placid and tireless — hitch it to the work, walk away, and it just finishes.
Good for steady reliable effort that needs no minding, and delegation and
automation.
