# Boss — character pack

A round buckler shield. The name reads off the part — the **boss** is the
literal dome at a shield's center — and doubles for the fights worth having.

Credit: **Boss by Trevin Chow**.

Style: **enamel**
Aliases: shield, buckler

## Locked design

- **Body**: one round, slightly domed buckler shield standing upright — a
  single circular silhouette. Every part is a closed cell bounded by the
  metal line.
- **Face**: two simple dot eyes set high on the shield face, as small pale
  enamel cells. Completely blank, deadpan, neutral — **no eyebrows, no
  mouth, ever**.
- **Accent carrier**: the round dome bump at the shield's center (the boss)
  — the only accent-colored part, and it must visibly contrast with the
  body.
- Small stubby arms at the sides and stubby legs underneath.
- Cuteness comes from proportion and roundness, never from added parts.
- Never add: heraldry, patterns, rivets, straps, weapons, a second dome,
  eyebrows, mouth, clothing, accessories.

## Prompt spec

> the recurring mascot — one round, slightly domed buckler shield standing
> upright on small stubby legs, two simple dot eyes set high on the shield
> face as pale enamel cells, blank deadpan (no eyebrows, no mouth), small
> stubby arms; at the shield's center one round dome bump — the ONLY
> accent-colored part, in accent enamel that must NOT be the body color. It
> MUST perform the move, not decorate. Every part, eyes included, is a
> closed cell bounded by the metal line. {value rule: deep enamel body with
> pale eyes, or pale body with deep eyes on light treatments}

## Value rules

Enamel maps value to cell fills (see the style's palette mapping):

- **Default (dark-capable)**: deep enamel body in the structure hue, pale
  enamel eyes; metal outlines follow the structure ink's metal mapping
  (near-black → black-nickel, warm → antique gold).
- **Light treatment**: pale enamel body with deep enamel eyes.
- The dome always takes the palette accent — **force the contrast in the
  prompt** ("must NOT be the body color"); stated plainly, the model drops
  the accent into the body enamel.

## Staging notes

Enamel shines in emblematic and discrete-station scenes — deflections,
gates, badges, step-by-step stations — and discretizes continuous paths into
floating cells. Pick shots accordingly (the style file's "Staging fit"
section is the rule).

## Personality

The unbothered last line — plants its feet, takes the hit, asks if that's
all. The character for security, guarding prod, defense-in-depth, hard
challenges, and boss fights.
